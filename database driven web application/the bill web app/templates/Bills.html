Bills Analysis

A bill's life cycle in the legislature procedure

  • A bill is first introduced (sponsored) by the a legislator
  • Then the bill is assigned to one or more related committees in the house or the senate
  • After the committee decides that the bill be suitable for a chamber vote, a report is ordered by the committee to the house/senate
  • The bill passes the house/senate vote, the form of which varies and include "roll call vote" where the voting details are recorded
  • Then the bill is to be passed the vote of the other part of the congress
  • Signed by the president and be enacted.
