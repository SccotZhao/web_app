Background Introduction

This website is based on data fetched from the US congress website which can be found here. The goal of this website is to enable the user to get a big picture analysis of bills that will be introduced during the 115th congress . Some of functionalities it will provide will be an analysis of number of bills introduced by different parties, under different sectors over time, the voting patterns of bills by their sectors, determining if there are clear voting trends for bills in certain categories based on the party affiliations of the members of congress. Users will also have the ability to get a general analysis of the voting trend for a particular member of congress(House Representative or senator) or get summary analysis such as the member who votes 'No" or "Yes" most times. A general comparison of two members will also be possible. The site will mainly focus on the sectors that Trump's campaign was centered around such as immigration, foreign policy, environment and energy. By exploring the voting patterns, we want to explore if members of congress vote strictly along bipartisan lines or if there are other influencing the bills
