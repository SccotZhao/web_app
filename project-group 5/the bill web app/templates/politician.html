Member Analysis

The US congress is composed of the Senate and the House of Representatives. Congress has 535 voting members: 435 Representatives and 100 Senators. Besides, the House has six non-voting members representing Washington D.C., Puerto Rico, American Samoa, Guam, the Northern Mariana Islands, and the U.S. Virgin Islands. The congressmen are mainly affiliated to the Republican Party or to the Democratic Party, and rarely to a third party or as independents. Committees are formed to focus on the particular sectors of legislation.
